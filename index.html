Medium

Medium influences content.

  • Friction: Difficulty to produce
  • Speed: Time to produce
  • Reach: Size of audience
  • Quality: Control of output

Print

  • Expensive to mass publish & distribute
  • Time consuming, often involving many trades and processes
  • Audience only as big as number printed
  • Exact control over output

Web

  • Inexpensive to mass publish & distribute
  • Quick, immediate publishing
  • Potentially global audience
  • Moderate control over output
With it's dynamic nature & the lack of robust layout and typographic control we've been forced to compromise on the output.

multi-column

In typography, a column is one or more vertical blocks of content positioned on a page, separated by gutters or rules. Columns are most commonly used to break up large bodies of text that cannot fit in a single block of text on a page. Additionally, columns are used to improve page composition and readability. Newspapers very frequently use complex multi-column layouts to break up different stories and longer bodies of texts within a story. Column can also more generally refer to the vertical delineations created by a typographic grid system which type and image may be positioned.
